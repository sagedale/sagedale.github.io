Field biologist, raptor researcher,
and conservation geneticist.

I am a PhD student in Ecology, Evolution, and Behavior at Boise State University, working in the Conservation Genetics Lab under Dr. Stephanie Galla. My work takes me to some of the most remote corners of the planet — the Arctic tundra — where I study how birds respond to environmental challenges at the molecular level.

My research sits at the intersection of ecology, immunology, and genomics. I am particularly interested in how host–pathogen dynamics shape genetic diversity in Gyrfalcons across their range and how mechanisms of immune competence underlie differential susceptibility to disease in wild populations.

Before starting my PhD, I received my BSc in Zoology from Oregon State University and my MS in Raptor Biology from Boise State University. Outside of research, I love to putter in the outdoors, bike and ski, knit, doodle, and spend time with my dog Jack.
